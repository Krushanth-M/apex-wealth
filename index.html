Best techniques to settle multiple debts faster:

1. Avalanche Method

Pay off debts with the highest interest rate first. Saves you the most interest money overall.

2. Snowball Method

Pay off debts with the smallest balance first. Gives you quick mental wins to stay motivated.

Financial Literacy Lessons

Learn these core financial principles in simple English to improve your money management.

The 50/30/20 Rule
A simple formula to manage your money: Spend 50% of your earnings on Needs (rent, food, bills), 30% on Wants (entertainment, eating out), and save the remaining 20% for future goals.
Emergency Fund
Save enough money to cover 3 to 6 months of your family's basic needs. Keep this cash in a safe bank account. Use it only for real emergencies like medical bills or job loss.
Compound Interest
When you invest, your money earns returns. In the next period, you earn interest on both your initial money and the returns already earned. Over time, your money grows exponentially!
Good Debt vs Bad Debt
Good debt is money borrowed to build long-term value, like a home loan or education. Bad debt is borrowing for everyday costs or luxury items, like buying clothes on a credit card.
SIP Mutual Funds
A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed small amount (like ₹500 or ₹1,000) every month. This helps you build wealth over time without timing the stock market.
Insurance First
Protect your family before investing. Get a Term Life Insurance to secure your family's future if you're not around, and Health Insurance so medical bills don't wipe out your savings.
Inflation
Inflation is the rate at which costs rise. If inflation is 6%, an item costing ₹10,000 will cost ₹10,600 next year. Your savings must grow faster than inflation to keep buying power.
Credit Score
A score that rates how responsibly you pay back loans. Keeping a clean repayment record builds a high score, which allows you to borrow money easily at lower interest rates.
